Isle Royale National Park is a rugged, isolated island wilderness located in the remote waters of northwest Lake Superior. Spanning over 45 miles in length, this car-free sanctuary is renowned for its dense boreal forests, pristine interior lakes, and iconic wildlife populations—most notably its shifting predator-prey dynamic of timber wolves and moose.

Accessible only by ferry, seaplane, or private boat, the island challenges backpackers with hundreds of miles of technical footpaths, ridge-line views along the Greenstone Ridge, and a network of 34 secluded backcountry campgrounds. Whether you are navigating a cross-island thru-hike or planning a quiet inland canoe portage, mapping your route meticulously is essential for a safe, unforgettable wilderness experience.
